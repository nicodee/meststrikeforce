Some useful tips

Important: edit and proofread before hitting "Send." You only get one chance to make a good first impression, so make it count. :)

Use simple English, and say exactly what you mean. Aim for clarity, not impressiveness.

Put your most important point in the opening sentence or paragraph. If you put it in the middle or the bottom, it might not get seen.

Always enter a descriptive subject line. For example, instead of "Logo," try "Follow-up to logo design brainstorm"

Be clear about what you’re asking. Being straight-forward makes it easy for the mentor to help you.

Link to referenced documents or resources whenever possible. Don’t simply say “Kindly refer to the marketing plan document.” Instead, provide a link to that document. Make things as easy as possible for your reader.

Avoid using ALL CAPITALS - some people consider them to be the email equivalent of shouting.

As a general rule, PLZ avoid textspeak (abbreviations and acronyms): you may be ROFLOL (rolling on the floor laughing out loud), but your reader may be left wondering WUWT (what's up with that).

Be brief and polite. Remember to say “Please” and “Thank you.”

If your message runs longer than two or three short paragraphs, consider (a) reducing the message, or (b) providing an attachment.

Add a signature block with appropriate contact information (in most cases, your name, business address, and phone number).

Reply promptly to serious messages. If you need more than 24 hours to collect information or make a decision, send a brief response explaining the delay.

Stick to one topic for each email.

Avoid excessive compliments.

Always, always proofread email before sending!
